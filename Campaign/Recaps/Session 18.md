## Underground Fight Club

After descending into the rowdy underground establishment known as [[The Floating Chair]], the party made their way toward the fighting pit, a tiered arena descending twenty-five feet into a sand-covered floor, dominated by five stone towers of varying height. The tail end of a warm-up match played out before them: two fighters bringing down an owlbear to a mix of cheers and frustrated groans from the crowd.

Borgemir spotted their contact first. [[Thorim Daybreaker]], the tournament bookie, was perched on a small stool just to see over the heads around him. The dwarf paladin greeted Thorim like an old friend, dapping him up. However Thorim's demeanor shifted, when he turned to Borgemir's unfamiliar companions. Luffy, disguised as a warrior from the far-eastern land of Slane, was eventually recognized, not from the pits, but from a blackjack table where the dragonborn had once tried to cheat Thorim. 

After clearing up the party member of [[The Drunken Gamblers]] Thorim began explaining the rules of the pit. Outside the arena, orderly manners was expected, but Inside it, there were no rules at all, any spell, weapon, or tactic was permitted, and the victor alone decided whether the vanquished lived or died. Fighters would be lowered into the pit through separate hatches with little time to prepare. Between brackets, combatants were given an hour-long rest to recover. During this Borgemir's asked whether he could "remove things from opponents body", which was met with suspicion, but allowed due to the free for all rules. 

The betting rules were straightforward: fighters could wager on their own victory but never against themselves, with a maximum of fifty gold per fight and winnings eligible to roll forward into later rounds. The odds on their opening match against the Blood Brothers sat at roughly 60/40 in the party's favor. Prize money scaled with the bracket: five gold per member for a first-round win, fifteen for the second, and for the tournament champion, fifty gold, a mysterious sponsored trophy, and the right to select a weapons from the armory. The group pooled their funds and placed a combined wager of seven gold and two silver on themselves. 

Through careful observation and eavesdropping, the party pieced together the bracket. Their opponents, the Blood Brothers, were an imposing goliath named Ogref wielding spear and shield, and a half-orc called Samson carrying a greatsword, undefeated veterans known for an aggressive, reckless fighting style. Across the bracket stood The Grey Swords, a pair of seasoned human warriors in their late forties or fifties, each armed with longsword and shortsword, one of whom was missing his left eye. The crowd's betting chatter confirmed them as heavy favorites and fan champions. The final entry, "Monster Amalgams," was not a team of fighters at all, it was a wildcard monster supplied by the arena itself. 

One detail nagged at the party. Many of the wealthier spectators wore masquerade masks, and Luffy discovered an unnatural difficulty when focusing on their faces. It wasn't simple anonymity, a magical force actively diverted his attention, preventing recognition. These enchanted masks clearly allowed high-profile figures to frequent the semi-legal pits without risking their reputations.

## The Drunken Snatchers

Their platform descended into the arena, revealing not just their opponents but hidden traps, pitfall mechanisms surrounding certain flagstones and perimeter chains triggering poison dart volleys from the towers.

Caelum's opening _Hold Person_ spell failed against the goliath's willpower, drawing laughter from the crowd as his follow-up crossbow shot went wide. Borgemir's _Bless_ bolstered the party's spirits before he mounted his warhorse Freya and took defensive position. Their winged companion Symph flew to the top of one of the towers where he discovered strange holes covering the top of the towers. 

The Blood Brothers demonstrated terrifying speed, closes the distance with reckless aggression and surrounds Caelum. Despite the grim situation, Caelum keeps his head clear and unleashes a third-level _Hold Person_ targeting both Ogref and Samson. While Ogref resisted, the wounded Samson froze completely. Caelum's subsequent blinding arrow hampered the goliath. Luffy delivered a detvisating blow, followed up by Borgemir charging in on his mount and attacking the paralyzed half-orc. The mace shattering his leg with two devastating critical non-lethal blows.

Upon seeing his brothers fall the The goliath becomes enraged and unleashed a three-strike combination against Caelum that would have proven fatal, but the bard's _Cutting Words_ disrupted the first swing while blindness spoiled the remaining attacks. Together the party fought the enraged goliath with Luffy critical dagger strike brought the giant to his knees, and Caelum's final crossbow bolt to the kneecap, deliberately incapacitating rather than killing, ended the fight.

What followed transcended mere victory. While Caelum pleaded against further brutality, Borgemir proceeded with his signature humiliation, removing one testicle from each of the unconscious brothers. Borgemir, seizing the moment of spectacle, held both testies aloft and announced an auction for them after the next fight. The crowd's response was rapturous, they showered him with silver coins and a new title: "the ball-snatcher." Caelum, ever practical, collected three gold worth of thrown coins while Borgemir basked in reflected glory.

Between fights, the group successfully auctioned off the collected trophies to the crowd for a handsome 14.5 gold. The party also collected their five-gold-per-head prize and their betting winnings, then immediately let 21 gold and 7 silver ride on themselves for the next round.

During the downtime, Luffy scanned the wealthy box seats and caught the feeling of recognition. A Dragonborn among the masked spectators sparked something deep and inside him. But the enchantment on the masks held firm, and the feeling slipped away before he could pin it down.

## The Gray Swords

Between rounds, the party witnessed the Gray Swords' tactical mastery against the regenerating Cave Troll. The veteran gladiators used the arena's geography to lure the beast into the spike pit, and severing its limbs. . The display was both awe-inspiring and sobering, these were the opponents awaiting them after the fight.

Their  match against the Gray Swords began with Luffy's _Faerie Fire_ spell failing against the older warriors. One brother vanished behind the corner tower while his companion engaged. Borgemir sent Freya to flush out the hidden opponent; the horse succeeded but took a deep sword wound for her courage, retaliating with a distinctive hoofprint on the warrior's armor before teleporting to safety.

The party committed their full resources when the situation demanded it. Caelum's _Hypnotic Pattern_ charmed one brother into staring helplessly at colorful lights, while Borgemir's mounted charge delivered a _Hold Person_ spell that paralyzed the other. Luffy's critical dagger thrust, follow-up by Symph's pulling of the chain, caused the poisonous darts to fly from the wall eliminating the first fighter. For the charmed brother, the party devised a grimly creative end. They maneuvered the dazed warrior to the edge of an open spike pit and shoved him in. When the fall broke the charm, it was too late, Luffy rained daggers from above, and then  stepped off the pressure plate. The stone lid ground shut, crushing the trapped fighter completely. A sickening crack echoed through the arena as the crowd roared with approval.

Their winnings, 15 gold prize plus doubled bets totaling over 43 gold, where 20 were immediately wagered again on themselves for the final.

## The Flood

The grand final began with betrayal by the arena itself. As the party stood in the center awaiting their opponents, grates along the walls opened and water thundered into the pit, ten feet deep, transforming the sandy floor into a churning deathtrap where only the tower tops remained as islands. Forced to abandon their footing, the Drunken Gamblers treaded water as their preparations proved inadequate for aquatic combat.

From the depths below, a massive gate ground open. A shadowy figure shot upward with unnerving speed, swimming beneath their feet.
